Beginner Muscle Gain Trainer

A straightforward guide to packing on muscle, designed specifically for slim beginners. Follow the plan, eat heavy, and stay consistent.

Weekly Training Plan

Bench Press Monday - Chest
Sets: 3 | Reps: 8-10

The king of upper body exercises to build a massive chest.

Target Muscles: Pectorals, Triceps, Shoulders.

Proper Form Tips:
  • Keep your feet planted flat on the floor.
  • Maintain a slight arch in your lower back.
  • Lower the bar to your mid-chest under control.
  • Push up powerfully, squeezing the chest.
Lat Pulldown Tuesday - Back
Sets: 3 | Reps: 10-12

Creates the "V-taper" look by widening your upper back.

Target Muscles: Latissimus Dorsi (Lats), Biceps.

Proper Form Tips:
  • Grip the bar slightly wider than shoulder-width.
  • Lean back slightly and puff your chest out.
  • Pull the bar down to your upper chest.
  • Focus on pulling your elbows down into your back pockets.
Squats Wednesday - Legs
Sets: 3 | Reps: 8-10

The ultimate foundation builder for overall body mass and leg strength.

Target Muscles: Quadriceps, Hamstrings, Glutes, Core.

Proper Form Tips:
  • Keep your chest up and core tight the entire time.
  • Push your hips back as if sitting in a chair.
  • Push your knees outward so they do not cave in.
  • Drive through your heels to stand back up.
DB Shoulder Press Thursday - Shoulders
Sets: 3 | Reps: 8-10

Builds broad, rounded shoulders to widen your upper frame.

Target Muscles: Deltoids (Shoulders), Triceps.

Proper Form Tips:
  • Sit on a bench with a backrest for support.
  • Press the dumbbells straight up above your head.
  • Do not aggressively arch your lower back.
  • Lower them slowly until they are level with your ears.
Curls & Pushdowns Friday - Arms
Sets: 3 each | Reps: 10-12

Isolates the biceps and triceps to stretch your t-shirt sleeves.

Target Muscles: Biceps, Triceps.

Proper Form Tips:
  • Bicep Curls: Pin your elbows to your sides. Do not swing your body.
  • Tricep Pushdowns: Use a cable rope. Keep elbows stationary and push hands down until arms lock out.
Recovery & Rest Weekend
Saturday & Sunday

Your muscles do not grow in the gym; they grow when you rest and eat.

Target: Central Nervous System & Muscle Repair.

Weekend Protocol:
  • Saturday: Light 15-minute stretching or walking to keep blood flowing.
  • Sunday: Absolute rest. Prepare your meals for the week.

Muscle Gain Diet Guide

To gain weight, you must be in a caloric surplus. Focus on these high-calorie, low-prep foods that are easy to manage in a hostel setting.

  • 🥚 Eggs & 🥛 Milk

    The absolute gold standard for cheap, accessible protein. Grab extra eggs from the mess, or boil them yourself in an electric kettle. Drink full-fat milk daily to easily bump up your calorie intake.

  • 🍌 Banana Shake & 🥜 Peanut Butter

    Peanut butter is the ultimate cheat code for slim guys—it requires no refrigeration and is packed with calories and healthy fats. Blend or mix bananas, milk, and a huge scoop of peanut butter for an instant mass gainer shake.

  • 🍗 Chicken / 🧀 Paneer

    Whenever the canteen or mess serves chicken or paneer, prioritize eating massive portions of it. These are your heavy-hitting protein sources for muscle repair.

  • 🥣 Oats

    Complex carbs are the fuel for your workouts. Mix oats with warm milk and a spoon of peanut butter for a high-energy, heavy breakfast before classes.

Beginner Tips for Success

  • 🔥 Warm Up Before Workouts

    Never walk in and lift heavy immediately. Spend 5-10 minutes doing arm circles, light jogging, or lifting just the empty bar to get blood into the joints.

  • 📐 Focus on Proper Form

    Leave your ego at the door. Lifting lighter weights with perfect technique will build much more muscle than lifting heavy weights with terrible, swinging form.

  • 📈 Progressive Overload

    To keep growing, your workouts must get harder over time. Aim to either lift slightly more weight or do one extra rep than you did the previous week.

  • 💤 Sleep 7–8 Hours

    Balancing AMU classes and grinding for the DSSSB ASO exam is exhausting, but you cannot skip sleep. Your brain needs it for studying, and your muscles physically repair themselves only during deep sleep.

  • 💧 Drink Enough Water

    Keep a water bottle on your desk at all times. Aim for 3-4 liters a day, especially if you are taking supplements like creatine.
